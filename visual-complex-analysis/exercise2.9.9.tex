\begin{exercise}[2.9]
Here is an attempt at using real methods to expand
$H(x)=\frac{1}{1+x^2}$ into a power series centered at $x=k$,i.e,into
a series of the form $H(x)=\sum_{j=0}^{\infty}c_jX^j$,where
$X=(x-k)$.According to Taylor's theorem,$c_j=\frac{H^{(j)}(k)}{j!}$.
\end{exercise}
